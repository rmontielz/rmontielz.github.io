CC3. Debating. Use a visualisation in economic/policy commentary

This section is inspired by the panel "The kids aren’t alright: Tackling a legacy of child poverty, bad diets and poor health".

The first graph shows UK childhood obesity rising above a stable global average. The second graph highlights how eating healthy is less affordable for lower-income families.

CC4. Replication. Re-create, then improve, someone else’s chart.

I recreated and refined the chart from Food Foundation by combining two graphs into a single, streamlined visualization, reducing unnecessary "ink" for better clarity. I corrected a meaningless color variation in the original and removed the redundant repetition of the word "year" by incorporating it into the axis label.

CC5. Scraper. Implement a data scraper of your own.

I used Pandas and the Banco Central de Chile's recently introduced API to scrape Gross Fixed Capital Formation data. Click to see the Python code I used. I chose it because the Chilean Central Bank is an institution from which I will frequently need data, and learning its API is highly useful.
